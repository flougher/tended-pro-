ℹ️ About Black Box Warnings

A Black Box Warning (also called a boxed warning) is the FDA's most serious drug safety warning. It means the medication has a known risk of serious or life-threatening adverse effects. As a caregiver, your role is to monitor for the listed symptoms and report them immediately to the supervising nurse or physician — never adjust or withhold medication without an order.

💾 Data Management
📤 Back Up Your Data

Tapping Export All Data saves a complete backup of everything in the app — all patients, medications, MAR records, incidents, care plans, wounds, behaviors, billing, vitals, pain logs, and credentials — as a single .json file on your device.

What to do with the file:
• Save it to Google Drive or your phone's Downloads folder
• Email it to yourself as a personal backup
• Save it before switching phones or reinstalling the app
⏰ Recommended: Export at least once a week, or after every shift if documenting critical care.
📥 Restore from Backup

Tapping Import Backup lets you select a previously exported .json file and restores all your data — patients, records, and settings — exactly as they were when you exported.

When to use it:
• You got a new phone and need to transfer your data
• You reinstalled the app and lost your records
• You accidentally cleared the app's storage
• You want to restore an older backup
⚠️ Important: Importing a backup will merge with your existing data. New entries in the backup file will be added — existing records will not be deleted. Always export your current data first before importing an older backup.
⚠️ Compliance Notice: Ensure device security meets your agency's HIPAA policies. This app stores data locally only — no cloud servers.

📖 Normal Reference Ranges (Adult)
❤️ Blood Pressure
Normal: <120/80
Elevated: 120–129/<80
High: ≥130/80
Crisis: >180/120
💓 Pulse
Normal: 60–100 bpm
Bradycardia: <60
Tachycardia: >100
🌡️ Temperature
Normal: 97–99°F
Low-grade: 99–100.4°F
Fever: ≥100.4°F
Hypothermia: <95°F
🫁 SpO2
Normal: 95–100%
Low: 91–94%
Critical: <90%
🩸 Blood Glucose
Fasting: 70–100 mg/dL
Post-meal: <140 mg/dL
Hypo: <70 mg/dL
Hyper: >180 mg/dL
🫀 Respirations
Normal: 12–20/min
Bradypnea: <12/min
Tachypnea: >20/min
⚕️ Reference ranges are general guidelines for adults. Always follow your patient's physician-ordered target ranges, which may differ. Report out-of-range values to your supervisor immediately.
